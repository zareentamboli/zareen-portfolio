03 — Extracurricular & Community

Where values meet
action

Two initiatives that shaped how I think about governance, equity, and the responsibility of design — both with institutional weight behind them.

⚖️
Government Mandate · University Governance
Women's Grievances & Redressal Committee
Mandated by the Government of India under the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace Act (POSH) · Pune University

India's POSH Act 2013 requires every educational institution to maintain an Internal Complaints Committee — and I served as a student representative on ours for three years. This was not a ceremonial role.


I handled formal complaints submitted by students, assessed them in line with government-prescribed procedures, maintained strict confidentiality, and participated in structured institutional hearings. The process demanded procedural precision, impartiality, and emotional intelligence in equal measure.


Beyond individual cases, I contributed to awareness initiatives to create a more informed, equitable campus culture — bridging the gap between policy and lived experience. This experience gave me a deep understanding of institutional governance, legal compliance frameworks, and the human dimension of systemic change — skills directly transferable to ESG and sustainability reporting in a UK professional context.

Aug 2021 – Jun 2024 · 3 years
🌾
Govt. of India Initiative · Social Impact Design
Unnat Bharat Abhiyan
Flagship Programme · Ministry of Education, Government of India · Rural Maharastra

Unnat Bharat Abhiyan (meaning "Enlightened India Campaign") is a flagship initiative of India's Ministry of Education, connecting higher education institutions with rural clusters to drive sustainable rural development. It is one of India's most significant government-led university-community engagement programmes.


I was selected to design and build a children's play space for an adopted rural village — constructed entirely from upcycled and locally-sourced materials. The brief was simple: make it safe, sustainable, joyful, and buildable by the community. The process was not.


Working with village residents, local craftspeople, and my university team, I applied circular economy principles and green infrastructure thinking to a real-world, resource-constrained environment. It was the project that taught me sustainability only counts when it reaches the people who need it most — a conviction that drives my approach to every project since.

07 — My Life

Beyond the
CV

"I grew up between construction sites and classrooms — learning that the built environment shapes lives before I had the words to describe why it mattered."
🏡
Roots in Pune
I grew up in Pune — a city of universities, culture, and a thriving architectural scene. My childhood was shaped by Alpine Realty, my family's real estate business, where dinner conversations turned to planning permissions, construction costs, and what makes a building stand the test of time. That early immersion gave me a rare blend of creative and commercial instinct.
🌍
Moving to London
Coming to London to study at UCL was both a professional and personal leap. The city's scale, diversity, and obsession with its own architecture never gets old. I've explored London's green spaces — from the Barbican's brutalist garden to the ecology along the Lea Valley — and find the city's struggle to retrofit itself sustainably genuinely fascinating. This is exactly the challenge I want to work on.
📚
How I think
I'm drawn to problems that sit at the intersection of the technical and the human. I read widely — urban policy, climate economics, architecture criticism — and believe that the best sustainability solutions come from understanding why people make the choices they do, not just optimising the buildings around them. That curiosity makes me a better consultant.
🤝
Community & service
Serving on Pune University's Women's Grievances Committee for three years taught me that institutional change requires patience, precision, and genuine care for people — not just good intentions. That experience sits alongside designing a rural play space under Unnat Bharat Abhiyan. Service isn't something I do alongside my career — it's woven into how I think about the purpose of design.
🎨
Creative & visual
Architecture trained me to see space differently — to notice how light falls, how materials age, how a room makes people feel. That visual sensibility shows up in how I communicate technical work: I believe a well-crafted diagram or visualisation can persuade a client more effectively than a hundred pages of report. Proficient in Rhino, Lumion, Enscape, Figma, Photoshop, and InDesign.
🚀
What's next

I bring technical depth, commercial grounding, and a genuine passion for the UK's green transition. I'm on the Graduate Route, available immediately, and ready to contribute from day one.
